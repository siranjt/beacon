Chapter I

The Beacon.

A fixed point that doesn't move when the world does.

The harbor is old. The stones are weathered. The storm tonight is the same storm as a hundred years before, and the lighthouse has stood through every one of them.

Every great work needs a beacon. A place that holds. A frame the rest of the system rests against when the wind picks up.

Beacon is the platform — six umbrella agents woven into one. Customer. Performance. Escalation. Post-Payment. Miss-Payment. Negative Keyword. One launcher. One sign-in. One ground that doesn't shift.

Chapter II

The flame.

Small. Patient. Watchful.

Atop the beacon burns a flame. It moves with the wind. It bends. It flickers. It does not go out.

The flame is what makes a beacon a beacon. Without it, there is only stone — a tall building on a cliff, telling no one anything.

The flame doesn't speak. It doesn't decide. It is the source. It is the thing that turns weather into warning, dark into bearing.

Chapter III

The beam.

It does not decide. It does not remember. It reveals.

From the flame comes the beam. A sweeping cone of warm light, cutting across the rain, falling on the rocks, the sea, the cliffs — illuminating, for one moment, what the dark was hiding.

Then it moves on.

Ask Beam a question, and it shows you what it sees, the moment you ask. It does not pretend to remember. It does not pretend to judge. It reveals — clean, fast, cited.

Chapter IV

The Keeper.

A beam without a keeper is just light pointing nowhere.

Pan down. At the base of the lighthouse, half in shadow, half in the warm glow of the flame above — a figure. A long cloak, fur-lined against the rain. A sword at the hip. The hood drawn against the wind.

The Keeper is not the beacon. Not the flame. Not the beam. The Keeper is the one who safeguards them all.

Without the Keeper, the flame would die in the first storm. The beam would forget what it last revealed. The story would be lost between sweeps.

The Keeper tends. The Keeper remembers. The Keeper stands watch.

How the Keeper decides what's true — when two facts collide

Math picks the winner. Recency × Confidence × Source Trust. Deterministic. Auditable. No LLM in the loop. Losers don't die — they stay, marked superseded_by, so the history is preserved.

What the Keeper does when the candidates come in — the Validate inbox

  • Haiku-extracted candidates land in a triage queue at /admin/brain/validate. AMs confirm, edit, reject, reclassify. Source quotes shown alongside every candidate.
  • Reject-rate dashboard at /admin/brain/reject-rate. Confirm / refine / reject / pending across 7d / 30d / all-time, per AM. Auto-callout when any window exceeds 30%.
  • derived_from relationships. When a parent fact gets superseded, every child fact derived from it is flagged for re-evaluation. Provenance follows the data.
  • Auto-prune stale facts. Anything older than its category's stale-horizon is quietly archived. The watch keeps moving forward.
Chapter V

The watch.

Beam gives answers. Keeper holds truth. Neither matters alone.

The flame above the harbor and the figure below have always been one thing. The beam without the Keeper goes dark by morning. The Keeper without the beam stands watch over an empty cliff.

This is how the watch works.

How the lighthouse works — the architecture, one diagram

A typical Customer 360 question takes 2–4 seconds end-to-end. Cold cache: 620ms of context fan-out. Hot cache: 120ms. The longest path is always Anthropic's streaming. Everything else runs parallel against it.

  • Wave 1 — How the Keeper hears. pgvector cosine (top 50) and tsvector match (top 50) run in parallel, fused by RRF (k = 60), reranked by Voyage rerank-2.5-lite. The right 5 of ~40 candidates land in Beam's context. Pure embeddings miss exact-token matches; pure keywords miss paraphrases. Hybrid does both.
  • Wave 2 — How the Keeper decides. When two facts collide, deterministic ranking picks the winner using the trust hierarchy above. Losers stay in the audit log via superseded_by. Beam only sees the winner by default. No LLM in the loop. Auditable.
  • Wave 3 — How the watch stays fast. Heavy context loaders (Customer 360, Customer Book, Post-Payment Book) cache their blobs for 5 minutes. Stage A refresh busts the cache the moment new data lands. AMs feel the snap on every follow-up question.
